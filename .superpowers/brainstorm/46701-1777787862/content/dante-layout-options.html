Which Dante Troubleshooting Layout?

Goal: admin opens the page and first sees whether Dante/PTP is healthy. Silence diagnosis, recent changes, and recovery are secondary paths.

A. Health Command Center
Top: large Health Verdict + PTP lock + Dante device + NIC
Middle: 4 triage lanes: Clock, Network, Audio Flow, Device
Bottom: route trace, events, recovery actions

A. Health Command Center

Best for “is this system healthy?” first. Keeps deeper cards available but demotes them.

B. Guided Troubleshooter
Top: pick problem: PTP, silent output, missing device, recent change
Main: selected workflow with checks and next actions
Side: raw diagnostics and event log

B. Guided Troubleshooter

Best for less expert admins. More opinionated, but risks hiding useful raw state.

C. Split Health + Inspector
Left: sticky health rail with verdicts and recovery
Right: dense inspector sections for PTP, network, subscriptions, trace, events
Mobile: health rail stacks above inspector

C. Split Health + Inspector

Best for expert troubleshooting. Strong hierarchy, but denser than option A.

My recommendation is A: health verdict first, then clear lanes for deeper troubleshooting. Click an option if one feels right, then respond in terminal.
